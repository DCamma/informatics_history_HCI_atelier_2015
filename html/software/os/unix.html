UNIX

Creation

Unix version 7

The creation of the operating system UNIX began as Bell Labs withdrew from the Multics (Multiplexed Information and Computing Service) project in 1969. Ken Thompson and Dennis Ritchie were among a few who had worked on the project and they were part of a small team that wanted to find an alternative operating system that included the desirable features of Multics. Thus began the creation of UNIX.

UNIX's Rise

Ken Thompson and Dennis Ritchie

The first edition of UNIX was released in 1971, it was used on a DEC PDP-7. UNIX's power and flexibility made it very popular, newer versions were released shortly after. A major breakthrough was made in the fourth edition of UNIX in 1973, as it was now being written in C, making it portable (it could be installed in almost any computer and translated to the language used in that computer). UNIX was also relatively inexpensive, which helped popularizing it as an operating system.

In 1975, UNIX version 6 was released: the first version that could be readily modified by a user. It used the Thompson shell, which would later inspire the interactive C shell.


Variations and Open Systems

During the 1980's, competition between different UNIX system products soared, resulting in many new versions of UNIX with diffrent features.

XENIX is one of the most well known variations of the UNIX system: it was created in 1980 by Microsoft. In the same year, BSD (Berkeley Software Distribution) released the fourth version of its own distribution of UNIX, called 4BSD. AT&T and Bell Labs split during this time period, dividing the UNIX system into different variations once again.

UNIX was futher popularized as X/Open Company Ltd used it as the basis of its open systems, which meet specific standards independent of the supplier. In 1987, AT&T along with Berkeley founded the OSF(Open Software Foundation), an open system. During the late 1980's there was immense competition between suppliers of AT&T's UNIX System V and OSF's UNIX based open systems. By 1991, a new company was formed: UNIX System Laboratories. Around this period, early versions of Linux (an operating system based on UNIX) and Solaris (a version of UNIX for SPARC) were being developed.
